Excel Showcase

I developed this excel sheet while solving a task in the module Technische Grundladen der Informatik at the University of Bremen. A phrase was given which had some missing digits. However because it was written on a level 5 raid protected harddrive, there is a possibility to restore the lost data by using XOR operations to calculate them again.

I decided excel was the way to go. I used the following excel functions in order to restore the missing digits:

  • hexindez()
    Converts a hex to a decimal
  • zeichen()
    Converts a decimal number to a ascii sign
  • hexinbin()
    Converts a hex to a binary
  • binindez()
    Converts a binary to a decimal

Click right to view the → Excel Tables

The XOR links of the digit groups have been calculated by following excel formula:

=WENN(REST(TEIL(M4;1;1)+TEIL(A4;1;1)+TEIL(E4;1;1);2)=1;1;0)&...

Eight of these terms, each connected to the next one with an &, now correctly give out the 8 digit bit string, which I wanted to restore. After that the bit strings have been morphed back to ascii signs in order to reveal the wanted sentence. Please note, that there are intended typos contained in the message to ensure, that the students don't just look up the sentence on the internet.

Copy Art

One of the topics in our first semester module Grundlagen der Gestaltung 1 was Copy Art. For quite some time we were drawing random lines on blank sheets of paper while listening to our favourite MP3s. After having finished, each of us had at least 4 to 5 sheets of more or less interesting figures and patterns. We then used a printer to scan some areas of these sheets and/or overlay them somehow to create additional substance and even more new forms and patterns. The white lines in the picture origin from the unimprinted border of the pages, which the printer can't reach.

Parody - Hirnfreie Demokraten

In the election campaigning of the FDP in Bremen, Germany, a candidate was chosen, that by no means was associated with the party. I won't discuss the reasons for this, as this is no political blog. However I was inspired by this bold action to make some kind of parody. Additionally the party just released new guidelines for the color palette and angles of the triangles in their campaign posters. Funnily they decided, that Cyan, Magenta, Yellow and Black have never been used together and are a good way to freshen up their conservative corporation-serving image. Perfect for me to make fun of that!
